## Translation Technologies (NSF SES-2336713)

My NSF-supported project investigates the cognitive, linguistic, and philosophical dimensions of modern translation technologies. Key themes include:

- **Human–machine interaction in translation.** How professional translators work with CAT tools, neural machine translation, and LLMs, and how these workflows redistribute cognitive labor.
- **Distributed cognition and extended mind.** Translation as a case study in technology-extended cognition.
- **Semantic opacity and neural attention.** What (if anything) NMT and LLM architectures reveal about meaning, interlingua, and cross-lingual alignment.
- **Translation quality evaluation.** Automatic metrics, human evaluation, and their correlation, with practical applications for freelance translators and small language service providers.

## Translation Analytics

A series of papers bringing state-of-the-art MT evaluation within reach of individual translators and small LSPs:

- **TA-I, TA-II, TA-III** — benchmarking commercial and local MT/LLM systems across language pairs (EN→DE, EN→RU, EN→ZH), with human evaluation, COMET–human correlation analyses, and confidential local-LLM workflows.
