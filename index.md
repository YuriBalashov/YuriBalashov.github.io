I am Professor of Philosophy at the [University of Georgia](https://www.uga.edu/), affiliated with the [Department of Philosophy](https://phil.uga.edu/) and the [Institute for Artificial Intelligence](https://www.ai.uga.edu/).

My current research, supported by the National Science Foundation (grant SES-2336713), sits at the intersection of philosophy of language, cognitive science, and artificial intelligence, with a focus on **translation technologies**: how professional translators interact with computer-assisted translation (CAT) tools, neural machine translation (NMT), and large language models (LLMs), and what this tells us about language, meaning, and distributed cognition.

I am also an [ATA-certified](https://www.atanet.org/) English-to-Russian translator and linguistic consultant, which keeps my research grounded in professional translation practice.
